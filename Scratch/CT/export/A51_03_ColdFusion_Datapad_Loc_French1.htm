The problem with traditional cold fusion
theory is that you get no bang for your
buck. Well, the real problem is that
palladium is too unstable to provide
consistent results, but even if it were
stable, you still only get about one-
tenth of a watt of energy per 100 watts
put into the process. Interesting, sure,
but not exactly mind-blowing.

So, with the Brannon system, you
eliminate this problem. Removing
deuterium oxide from the equation
turned out to be just the thing. I don't
know what they replaced it with, but
whatever it is generates a whole hell
of a lot more energy than deuterium
could ever hope to.
